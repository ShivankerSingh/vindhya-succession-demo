          Frameworks & Definitions

          Reference for the frameworks used across this dashboard · what they mean, how we use them, why they matter for Vindhya

          The three frameworks

          FL
          Future Leader
          Matrix: The 9-box · Performance × Potential

          What it answers: Who are the leaders demonstrating both high performance today AND the potential to grow into significantly bigger, more complex roles?

          The matrix: A 3×3 grid plotting each leader's current performance against future potential. Nine zones — Future Leader, Growth employee, Enigma, High impact, Core employee, Dilemma, Trusted professional, Effective, Underperformer. The top-right cell IS the Future Leader.

          What to look for: 8–15% in the Future Leader zone is a healthy range. Under 5% signals HiPo identification needs sharpening; over 20% suggests rating inflation.

          Vindhya context Given the asset-intensive manufacturing operation, the Trusted Professional zone is deep — the delivery backbone. The question is whether the Future Leader and Growth employee zones have enough density to fuel the growth agenda.
          SR
          Succession Readiness
          Matrix: Functional Readiness / Fit × Leadership Potential

          What it answers: For each critical role, who is the named successor pool — and how ready is each successor for that specific seat?

          The matrix: A 3×3 grid plotting each successor by Functional Readiness / Fit for the target role and their overall Leadership Potential. The cell maps directly to a readiness horizon — Ready Now (0–6 mo), Ready in 1 year, Ready in 2–3 years, or Ready in 4+ years.

          What to look for: Concentration of Ready-Now names (a healthy pipeline); over-concentration in Ready-in-2-3-years signals long-runway development needed at scale.

          Vindhya context Functional Fit is sourced from a company line-leader survey (leaders in or close to each target role); Leadership Potential comes from Aon's GLIDE assessment. High Functional Fit alone isn't enough for scale-up roles like BU CEO — high Leadership Potential is what enables growth.
          AI
          AI Readiness
          Matrix: Change Readiness × AI Use

          What it answers: How ready is the leadership to lead Vindhya's AI transition — behaviourally, and in terms of actual AI engagement?

          The matrix: A 3×3 grid mapping Change Readiness (Learning + Agile + Growth Mindset from personality data) against AI Use (training completion + tool usage + applied AI in work). Nine cells — AI Champion, Emerging Shaper, Confident User, Latent Shaper, Steady Adopter, Skilled Sceptic, Willing Follower, Reluctant User, At Risk.

          What to look for: The AI Champion base — under 10% is a concern for an organisation with an ambitious digital agenda.

          Vindhya context Directly ties to the stated AI transformation ambition in the strategic plan. A novel framework globally — first-mover advantage in taking it seriously.

          Inputs that feed the frameworks

          Aon and Vindhya each provide different pieces of data. Both are required for a complete picture.

          Inputs from Aon

          GL
          GLIDE — Aon's 3-lens view
          Feeds all three primary frameworks

          What it is: Aon's proprietary framework combining three lenses on a leader:

          • Personality — what they're naturally inclined to do (ADEPT-15)
          • Perception — how others see them (360° feedback)
          • Potential — what they can grow into (Development Centre observations)

          How it feeds: Potential (PT) feeds the 9-box and the Leadership Potential axis of the Readiness matrix. Personality (PN) inputs into AI Readiness (Change Readiness score). Triangulation across all three is more robust than any single source.

          A15
          ADEPT-15 — Personality dimensions
          An input into GLIDE's Personality lens

          What it is: Aon's 15-dimension personality assessment measuring stable behavioural preferences across three domains — Adjustment (how you handle emotion), Ambition (how you drive), and Sociability (how you engage others).

          How it feeds: Anchors the Personality lens of GLIDE, which then flows into all three primary frameworks. Cohort-level tilts on specific dimensions surface culture-level signals in Cohort Analysis.

          Inputs from Vindhya

          PR
          Performance ratings
          Feeds the Future Leader framework

          What it is: 2–3 years of consistent performance rating data across the Top-200, calibrated across BUs.

          How it feeds: Provides the Performance (X) axis of the 9-box. Without a consistent, comparable performance signal, the 9-box loses meaning.

          Vindhya-side owner: HR ops + BU HR heads for BU-level calibration.

          CR
          Critical role list + mapping
          Feeds the Succession Readiness framework

          What it is: The list of critical roles (Tier 1 / 2 / 3) + mapping of DC participants to each critical role as their target role(s). A candidate can be mapped to multiple target roles.

          How it feeds: Defines which roles the Succession Readiness matrix covers, and populates the successor pool per role.

          Vindhya-side owner: BU CEOs nominate; TM curates the mapping.

          FR
          Functional Readiness / Fit survey
          Feeds the Succession Readiness framework

          What it is: A structured survey (designed by Aon, completed by company line leaders who are in or close to each target role and familiar with the mapped candidates). Their view becomes the Functional Fit rating.

          How it feeds: Provides the Functional Readiness / Fit (Y) axis of the Succession Readiness matrix.

          Vindhya-side owner: BU CEOs + line leaders complete; TM administers.

          SP
          Project Ignite — AI use signals
          Feeds the AI Readiness framework

          What it is: Vindhya's internal AI enablement initiative — the ideal source for AI training completion, tool usage frequency, and applied-AI signals per leader.

          How it feeds: Provides the AI Use & Application (X) axis of the AI Readiness matrix. Where Ignite data is not yet available, manager-observed AI use is the fallback.

          Vindhya-side owner: Digital / AI team + LMS extract.

          Key terms

          Future Leader
          Top-right of the 9-box · High Performance × High Potential · the highest-value talent segment
          Top Successor
          A leader named on a Tier 1 or Tier 2 successor list — answers "who is stepping up in 12–24 months?"
          Ready Now
          Successor can step into the target role in under 6 months with only light development
          Ready in 1 year
          Needs 6–18 months of targeted development or a specific stretch experience
          Ready in 2–3 years
          Requires structured development plus a significant role or scope change
          Ready in 4+ years
          Long-runway development — new mental model or multiple passage changes needed
          Tier 1 / 2 / 3
          Role criticality — Tier 1 = enterprise-critical (Group CEO, CFO, BU CEOs), Tier 2 = BU/function-critical, Tier 3 = specialist-critical
          LA / LB / LC / LD
          Vindhya's career levels — LA = C-suite (Group CEO, BU CEOs), LB = SVP, LC = VP, LD = Senior Manager
          Healthy Pipeline
          Named Ready-Now successor and depth of at least 2 named successors
          Watch Pipeline
          Has a Ready-Now successor but only 1 name, or best successor is Ready-in-1-year
          At Risk Pipeline
          Best successor is Ready-in-2-3-years — no near-term coverage
          Exposed Pipeline
          No named successor, or only 4+ year readiness — role is materially vulnerable
          Flight Risk
          Likelihood of leader leaving the company — Low / Medium / High, based on tenure, engagement signals, external market activity
          Two-lens rule
          Every development action must be justified on both business need and individual growth — actions justified on only one need review
          AI Champion
          Leaders with High Change Readiness and High AI Use — the internal advocates for digital transformation
          Business Lever traits
          Trust & Loyalty, Cultural Synergy, Energy & Tenacity — assessed via Personality + 360° only (not Development Centre observation)

          Why these frameworks matter for Vindhya specifically

          1
          Family-led conglomerate structure
          Cross-BU visibility is often limited by BU-level talent decisions. The 9-box gives ExCo a group-wide view; the BU concentration lens surfaces single-source cohort risks; the Compare view enables informed decisions on cross-BU moves.
          2
          Manufacturing sector — capital-intensive with long asset lives
          Manufacturing plants, capital equipment and product platforms run for decades — often 15–30 years. Succession planning cannot be reactive — the readiness horizon framework (Ready Now → 4+ years) is a discipline for long-arc planning, not a short-term ranking.
          3
          Explicit AI / digital transformation agenda
          Vindhya's strategic plan calls out AI-driven operational efficiency across smart factories, quality control, supply-chain optimisation and predictive maintenance. The AI Readiness framework converts that ambition into a specific talent lens — who can actually lead the transition.
          4
          Decentralised BU structure with matrix moments
          Most decisions live within BUs, but Group-level moves (cross-BU rotations, Group-role successions) require different capabilities. The Succession Readiness matrix explicitly distinguishes Functional Readiness / Fit (can they do this specific role today?) from Leadership Potential (can they scale into complexity?) — critical for distinguishing stretch vs safe promotions.
